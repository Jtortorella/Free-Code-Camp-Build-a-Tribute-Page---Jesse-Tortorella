Theodore Roosevelt

President, Assemblyman, Nobel Peace Prize Winner and Author.

  • 1858 - Theodore Roosevelt was born to Theodore Roosevelt Sr. and Martha "Mittie" Bulloch Roosevelt. He was the second child of four children.
  • 1876 - Roosevelts enrolls into Harvard University. Over the next four years of undergrad, Teddy become involved in Boxing, Rowing and was editor of the Harvard Advocate.
  • 1880 - Roosevelt graduates from Harvard with a bachelor's degree in arts, magna cum laude.
  • 1880 - Roosevelt marries Alice Hathaway Lee, long-time girlfriend and sociallite.
  • 1880 - Roosevelts enrolls into Columbia Law School.
  • 1881 - Roosevelt drops out of Columbia Law School to pursuit politics.
  • 1881 - Roosevelt was elected to New York Assembly where he will stay elected for the next four years.
  • 1884 - Roosevelt and his wife, Alice, birth their first child, Alice Lee Roosevelt.
  • 1884 - Roosevelt's mother, Martha Roosevelt, dies of typhoid fever. In a sick twist of fate, hours later wife, Alice, is found dead due to undiagnosed typhoid fever.
  • 1886 - Theodore marries his second wife Edith Carow.
  • 1889 - Roosevelt takes position as the U.S. Civil Service Commissioner in Washington, DC. A position he will hold until 1895.
  • 1895 - Roosevelt is elected President of the Board of Police Commissioners. During that time, he recieves national praise for police reform in New York City.
  • 1897 - Roosevelt is appointed Assist Secretary of the Navy by President William McKinley.
  • 1898 - Roosevelt resigns as Assistant Secratary of the Navy to join the First U.S. Volunteer Cavalry Regiment. Theodore, within a year, recieves Lieutenant-Colonel commission and forms the "Rough Riders". By the end of 1898, Roosevelt was promoted to Colonel following serving in Cuba during the Spanish-American War.
  • 1900 - Following nomination by the Republican Party for Governor of New York State, Roosevelt wins the Governor's office and serves honorably until the end of the year.
  • 1900 - Theodore was nominated for Vice President alongside William McKinley. On November 6th, McKinley was elected President of the United States, Theodore was elected Vice-President.
  • 1901 - President McKinley is shot and later dies, making Roosevelt the 26th President. At that time, Roosevelt was the youngest president to serve.
  • 1902 - Theodore Roosevelt begins first of 45 antitrust suits to disolve monopolies. Theodore began establishing National Parks around the country starting with Crater Lake National Park in Oregon. Activities he would continue until the end of his presidency.
  • 1904 - Roosevelt is re-elected president. During his first two years, Roosevelt establishes the United States Forest Service and mediates the Russo-Japanese peace treaty between Japan and Russia.
  • 1906 - Roosevelt is awarded Nobel Peace Prize for mediating treaty of Russo-Japanese War.
  • 1909 - Roosevelt's presidency ends.
